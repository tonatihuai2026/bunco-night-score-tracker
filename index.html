How Bunco Scoring Works

Per-round points
Each round, a win is usually worth 1 point. Rolling three-of-a-kind on the round's target number ("a Bunco") is typically worth 21 points, and three-of-a-kind on any other number ("a mini-Bunco" or "baby Bunco") is usually worth 5 points. Add up a player's or table's total for the round and enter that single net number here.
Most points wins, most Buncos is a tiebreaker
At the end of the night, the player with the most total points usually wins, with most Buncos rolled as the standard tiebreaker. This tracker keeps the running point total per round so you always know where everyone stands — track Bunco counts separately on paper if your house rules use them as a tiebreaker.
House variants
Bunco scoring varies a lot table to table (point values for wins/losses/Buncos, win bonuses for a "Bunco Bonanza," carryover prizes). Whatever your table's rule, this tracker only needs the final net point number per player per round.
